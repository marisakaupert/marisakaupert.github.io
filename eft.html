Ball State Electronic Field Trips Website and Mobile App

Designer, Activity Developer, and Animator

Through the Digital Corps, the Ball State Electronic Field Trips Website and Mobile App was made for the state of Indiana's bicentennial celebration. The app is specifically aimed at teaching 4th grade history, and has been used in schools all over the state. Along with the app, a live broadcast was made at the Indiana State Museum and the original capital building in Corydon, Indiana.

For Electronic Field Trips, it was my job to help make various games and animation in the app. I designed and developed all the 'Measuring Map' games, which tests a student's estimation between various places in Indiana or places relating to Indiana history. I was also in charge of making games where the student was given a famous person in history, and they could customize their person with various accessories relating to their time period or relating to a theme. I built a tutorial on learning how to read a train schedule, and I made a game where students could analyze artifacts in Indiana history. These games were the ones I worked the most on, but I helped fix any bugs in all the games. In total, there are 79 games in the app. The app is available on the iTunes store or can be used online.

I helped with various motion graphics in the app, with my favorite being the loading screen animation. In the live broadcast, there was a song made about the history of Indiana. I helped animate a sequence of the song while also doing some clean-up animation at the end.
